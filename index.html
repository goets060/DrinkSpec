Our Solution to the Problem

We developed the DrinkSpec "Sentinel", a portable mass spectrometer designed to detect drink-spiking drugs in beverages. This solution was motivated by research that, in a survey of 6,064 students across three universities, 1 in 13 students reported being drugged or having their drinks spiked (Swan et al., 2017). By providing rapid, on-site chemical detection, Sentinel aims to empower individuals to verify the safety of their drinks and ultimately reduce the prevalence of drink spiking.

What does the DrinkSpec "Sentinel" do?

The DrinkSpec "Sentinel" acts a portable, bar-side chemical screening device. Its primary function is to detect and identify trace amounts of common drink-spiking agents such as GHB, ketamine, and benzodiazepines within a beverage. It is designed to serve as a definitive, rapid-response tool that replaces guesswork with chemical certainty.

How It Works & Phone Connectivity

The device operates using a minitaturized Mass Spectrometry-on-a-Chip architecture. The operational workflow involves sampling, analysis, connectivity, and integration.

Sampling

The user utilizes a single-use, biodegradable micro-straw to draw a microscopic droplet of the beverage into the device.

Analysis

The liquid is processed through a Micro-Electro-Mechanical System (MEMS) that performs "Atmospheric Pressure Chemical Ionization." This allows the device to operate without the bulky vacuum pumps required by traditional lab equipment.

Connectivity

The Sentinel utilizes Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) to pair instantly with the user's smartphone.

Integration

Once paired, the DrinkSpec app receives the data, processes it through a cloud-based algorithm to confirm if the chemical profile matches known illicit substances, and returns a result to the phone screen within 30 seconds.
